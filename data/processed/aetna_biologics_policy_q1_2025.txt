SECTION 1: TUMOR NECROSIS FACTOR (TNF) INHIBITORS

1.1 Adalimumab (Humira, Hadlima, Hyrimoz, Cyltezo, Yusimry)

Coverage Status: Covered with Restrictions
Tier: Specialty Tier 4
Quantity Limit: 2 prefilled syringes per 28 days (40 mg/0.8 mL)

Prior Authorization Required: YES

Prior Authorization Criteria:
1. Member must have an FDA-approved diagnosis: rheumatoid arthritis, psoriatic arthritis, ankylosing spondylitis, plaque psoriasis (moderate-to-severe), Crohn's disease, ulcerative colitis, or juvenile idiopathic arthritis.
2. Prescriber must be a rheumatologist, gastroenterologist, or dermatologist (or documentation of consultation).
3. Member must have tried and failed or have a documented contraindication to at least one conventional DMARD (e.g., methotrexate, sulfasalazine, leflunomide) for a minimum of 3 months.
4. Member must not have active tuberculosis or untreated latent TB (negative TB test required within 12 months).
5. Labs must include CBC, CMP within 6 months of authorization request.
6. For biosimilars: Aetna will preferentially authorize biosimilar adalimumab products before reference product (Humira). Documentation of clinical reason required if reference product requested.

Step Therapy Required: YES
Step 1: Conventional DMARDs (methotrexate, hydroxychloroquine, leflunomide, sulfasalazine) - minimum 3 months trial required with documented inadequate response or intolerance.

Age Restriction: 2 years and older (JIA indication); 18+ for other indications
Diagnosis Restriction: FDA-labeled indications only
Site of Care: Home self-administration preferred; infusion center requires step edit

---

1.2 Etanercept (Enbrel, Erelzi, Eticovo)

Coverage Status: Covered with Restrictions
Tier: Specialty Tier 4
Quantity Limit: 4 prefilled syringes per 28 days (50 mg/mL)

Prior Authorization Required: YES

Prior Authorization Criteria:
1. FDA-approved diagnosis required (RA, PsA, AS, plaque psoriasis, pJIA).
2. Specialty prescriber required (rheumatologist or dermatologist).
3. Must have failed at least one conventional DMARD (minimum 12 weeks) UNLESS contraindicated.
4. Negative TB screening within 12 months.

Step Therapy Required: YES
Step 1: Methotrexate monotherapy minimum 3 months.
Step 2: Combination DMARD therapy minimum 3 months (if Step 1 failed).

SECTION 2: INTERLEUKIN INHIBITORS

2.1 Dupilumab (Dupixent)

Coverage Status: Covered with Restrictions
Tier: Specialty Tier 4
Quantity Limit: 2 prefilled syringes per 28 days

Prior Authorization Required: YES

Prior Authorization Criteria:
1. Diagnosis of moderate-to-severe atopic dermatitis (EASI score ≥16, IGA score ≥3) not adequately controlled with topical therapies.
   OR diagnosis of moderate-to-severe asthma with eosinophilic phenotype or OCS-dependent.
   OR diagnosis of CRSwNP confirmed by CT or endoscopy.
2. Must have tried and failed two topical corticosteroids (medium-to-high potency) for atopic dermatitis, OR at least one inhaled corticosteroid for asthma.
3. Prescriber must be a dermatologist, allergist, or pulmonologist.
4. Eosinophil count must be ≥150 cells/µL for asthma indication.

Step Therapy Required: NO (for atopic dermatitis indication)
Age Restriction: 6 months and older (atopic dermatitis); 12+ (asthma)

---

2.2 Secukinumab (Cosentyx)

Coverage Status: Covered with Restrictions
Tier: Specialty Tier 4

Prior Authorization Required: YES

Prior Authorization Criteria:
1. FDA-approved diagnosis: plaque psoriasis, psoriatic arthritis, ankylosing spondylitis, non-radiographic axial spondyloarthritis, or enthesitis-related arthritis.
2. For plaque psoriasis: BSA ≥10% or DLQI >10, failed 2 topical agents.
3. For PsA or AS: failed one conventional DMARD or NSAID (6-week minimum).
4. Specialty prescriber required.

Step Therapy Required: YES (for PsA indication)
Step 1: NSAIDs minimum 4 weeks, then conventional DMARD minimum 3 months.

SECTION 3: GLP-1 RECEPTOR AGONISTS (Medical Benefit)

3.1 Semaglutide injection (Ozempic - diabetes; Wegovy - obesity)

Coverage Status: Covered (Medical Benefit - obesity management under medical supervision)
Tier: Specialty Tier 3

Prior Authorization Required: YES

Prior Authorization Criteria (Obesity Indication - Wegovy):
1. BMI ≥30 kg/m² OR BMI ≥27 kg/m² with at least one weight-related comorbidity (hypertension, type 2 diabetes, dyslipidemia, sleep apnea).
2. Must be enrolled in a comprehensive lifestyle intervention program (minimum monthly check-ins).
3. Must have tried and failed behavioral/dietary modification for at least 6 months with documented inadequate weight loss (<5% of body weight).
4. Prescriber must be an endocrinologist or obesity medicine specialist OR primary care provider with documentation of obesity management training.
5. Patient must not have a personal or family history of MTC or MEN2.

Prior Authorization Criteria (Diabetes Indication - Ozempic):
1. Diagnosis of Type 2 diabetes mellitus with HbA1c ≥7.5% despite metformin therapy (minimum 3 months at maximum tolerated dose).
2. Must have tried and failed metformin, OR metformin is contraindicated/not tolerated.
3. Prescriber must be a physician or NP/PA with documentation of diabetes management.

Step Therapy Required: YES (diabetes)
Step 1: Metformin - minimum 3 months at maximum tolerated dose, or documented intolerance/contraindication.
Step 2: Sulfonylurea added to metformin for minimum 3 months.
Age Restriction: 18+ (obesity); 12+ (type 2 diabetes - Ozempic)
Quantity Limit: 1 pen per 28 days (2.4 mg/dose for Wegovy; per dose titration schedule for Ozempic)

SECTION 4: ANTI-CD20 AGENTS

4.1 Rituximab (Rituxan, Riabni, Ruxience, Truxima)

Coverage Status: Covered with Restrictions
Tier: Specialty Tier 4

Prior Authorization Required: YES

Prior Authorization Criteria:
1. FDA-approved diagnosis: RA, GPA, MPA, CLL, NHL, pemphigus vulgaris.
2. For RA: must have failed or have contraindication to at least TWO DMARDs including methotrexate AND at least one TNF inhibitor (e.g., adalimumab, etanercept).
3. Infusion must be administered in a qualified infusion center or hospital outpatient setting with resuscitation equipment and trained personnel.
4. Premedication protocol (methylprednisolone 100 mg IV, acetaminophen, diphenhydramine) must be documented.
5. Hepatitis B screening required within 12 months.

Step Therapy Required: YES
Step 1: Methotrexate minimum 3 months.
Step 2: One additional DMARD minimum 3 months.
Step 3: One TNF inhibitor minimum 3 months with inadequate response.

Site of Care: Infusion center or hospital outpatient only. No home infusion.
Quantity Limit: Two 1,000 mg infusions per treatment course; maximum 2 courses per year for RA.

SECTION 5: GENERAL PROVISIONS

Coverage Exclusions:
- Drugs used for cosmetic purposes
- Investigational or experimental therapies
- Off-label use without peer-reviewed clinical evidence supporting efficacy
- Drugs prescribed by non-credentialed providers

Reauthorization Requirements:
All specialty biologics require annual reauthorization with documentation of:
- Clinical response (improvement in validated scoring tool)
- Absence of serious adverse events precluding continued therapy
- Continued medical necessity

Formulary Exception Process:
Members may request a formulary exception through the appeals process. Non-formulary drugs require demonstration of medical necessity and failure of all formulary alternatives.
